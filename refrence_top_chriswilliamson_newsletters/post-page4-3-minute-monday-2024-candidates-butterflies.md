​**Do voters discriminate against female candidates?**
“A meta-analysis of 67 experiments covering 120,601 people from six continents found that, in general, the answer is no.
On the contrary, people often prefer female candidates to otherwise identical males.
Breaking the results down by sex, women were strongly biased in favor of female candidates, whereas men were just a little biased in favor of female candidates.
The authors note that “Our results add to the growing body of experimental and observational evidence that voter preferences are not a major factor explaining the persistently low rates of women in elected office.” — Steve Stewart-Williams
